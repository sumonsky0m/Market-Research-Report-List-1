<p><p>Fluid Viscous Dampers (FVDs) are devices used to dissipate energy and reduce motion in structures, primarily during seismic events or vibrations. The market is segmented into linear and non-linear types. Linear FVDs exhibit a constant damping coefficient, providing predictable performance under varying loads. In contrast, non-linear FVDs have a variable damping response, adapting to different conditions for enhanced energy dissipation. Both types are integral in improving the resilience and stability of buildings and infrastructure against dynamic forces.</p></p>
