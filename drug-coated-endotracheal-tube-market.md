<p><p>The drug-coated endotracheal tube market includes various tube types tailored for specific clinical uses. Oral tubes are designed for placement through the mouth, while nasal tubes are inserted via the nose. Cuffed tubes feature an inflatable balloon to secure the airway, whereas uncuffed tubes do not. Preformed tubes are pre-shaped for ease of insertion, reinforced tubes possess added durability to prevent kinking, and double-lumen endobronchial tubes allow for ventilation of each lung independently. Each type addresses distinct patient needs in airway management.</p></p>
