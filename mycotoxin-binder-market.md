<p><p>The mycotoxin binder market comprises various types, each serving to mitigate mycotoxin contamination in animal feed and food products. Raw clay acts as a natural absorbent, effectively binding toxins and preventing their absorption in the gastrointestinal tract. Zeolites are crystalline aluminosilicates that trap mycotoxins through their unique framework. Aluminosilicates enhance binding efficiency due to their layered structure. Polysaccharides function by forming a gel-like matrix, which helps encapsulate mycotoxins and reduce their bioavailability, ensuring safer consumption of animal products.</p></p>
