<p><ul><li>Normal Pump</li><li>Patch Pump</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The insulin pumps market is primarily categorized into two types: normal pumps and patch pumps. Normal pumps are typically configured to be worn on a belt or clipped to clothing, offering programmable insulin delivery through a catheter. In contrast, patch pumps are discreet, adhesive devices that adhere directly to the skin, providing a wireless and less obtrusive option for insulin delivery. Both types aim to enhance diabetes management through precise and continuous insulin administration, improving patient comfort and control.</p></p>
