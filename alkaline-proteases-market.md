<p><p>The alkaline proteases market is primarily divided into serine proteases and metalloproteases, each with distinct characteristics and applications. Serine proteases utilize a serine residue in their active site, making them crucial in various biochemical processes, including digestion and protein metabolism. Metalloproteases, on the other hand, rely on metal ions for their catalytic activity and are significant in tissue remodeling and cellular processes. Both types are widely used in industries such as food processing, pharmaceuticals, and biotechnology.</p></p>
